Live Frosted-Glass Output

Beyond
binary.

QR codes encode data in black and white. Trixels encode in three states — dark, mid, light — mapped to the original image's hue and saturation. Your illustrations become the data. The data becomes your art.

Ternary Advantage
3⁶

729 states per codeword. GF(3⁶) Galois field arithmetic with Reed-Solomon error correction.

How it works

1

Encode

Feed a URL, text, or any data alongside your illustration. The Gaussian solver weaves your payload into a ternary matrix that preserves the image's visual fidelity.

2

Render

The HSL renderer maps each trit to the original pixel's hue and saturation. Typography gets frosted-glass immunity. Anchors calibrate the scanner.

3

Scan

Point any phone camera at the matrix. The on-device WASM decoder runs AnchorVision calibration, Reed-Solomon error correction, and recovers the payload in milliseconds.

Open standard. Open source.

Trixel is built in Rust with zero unsafe code and zero external solvers. The entire encode/decode pipeline — from GF(3⁶) arithmetic to the WASM camera scanner — ships as a single workspace of six crates.
